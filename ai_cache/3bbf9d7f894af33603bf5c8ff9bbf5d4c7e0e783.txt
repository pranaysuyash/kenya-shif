Implementation roadmap (high level)
- 0–6 months (stabilize, immediate fixes)
  - Define and publish a minimum PHC diagnostic and medicines formulary for each facility level.
  - Ring-fence portions of PCN global budgets for: essential medicines buffer, maternal referral transport, and rehabilitation starter kit.
  - Rapid training rollout: BP/glucose measurement, mhGAP basics, emergency obstetric stabilization.
  - Launch HMIS standardization and quarterly reconciliation SOPs.

- 6–24 months (scale & systems)
  - Scale chronic care registers, recall systems and telehealth consult links to referral hubs.
  - Strengthen county procurement and supply chain (pooled purchasing, buffer management).
  - Recruit or assign rehabilitation and allied health staff to referral hubs; pilot community-based rehab.
  - Establish ambulance pooling and formalized referral feedback loops for PCNs.

- 24–60 months (maturation & quality)
  - Integrate performance-based financing top-ups linked to outcomes (NCD control rates, maternal outcomes).
  - Full rollout of lab QA networks and digital imaging/tele-radiology.
  - Scale tailored models for pastoralist and slum populations; institutionalize CPD and staff retention measures.

Quick financing observations (policy lens)
- KES 900/person/year is a very constrained envelope for a comprehensive outpatient PHC package once NCDs, diagnostics, referral costs, workforce and rehabilitation are funded. Consider:
  - Graduated per-capita tariffs by risk/age (higher for elderly), or
  - Top-up performance-based funding for priority services, and
  - Ring-fencing for high-impact line items (maternal transport, essential NCD meds, diagnostics).
- Protecting free provision of ARVs, TB drugs and immunizations is critical, but integration requires supply chain alignment to avoid crowding-out.
